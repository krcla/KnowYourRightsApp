You Have Rights!

Regardless of immigration status, everyone has basic rights under the law. If you are approached by ICE or other law enforcement officers, it is important to know your rights.

Remain Silent
You have the right to remain silent. Do not answer any questions.
Do NOT Open Your Door
You do not have to open your door. To be allowed to enter your home ICE must have a warrant signed by a judge.
Representation
You have the right to speak to a lawyer. If you are detained, demand to speak with a lawyer.
Do NOT Sign
Do not sign anything without talking to a lawyer first.
Health & Family
If you are worried ICE will arrest you, let the officer know if you have children, a health condition or anything else that would cause your family to suffer if you are arrested. You have a right to medical treatment.
Report Trouble
You can show the officer this information on your phone or a Know Your Rights Card and call our 24-hour hotline at 1-844-500-3222.
